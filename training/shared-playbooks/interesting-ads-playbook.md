# Interesting Ads Playbook

## Core Principle

Ads need to be interesting. On social platforms, you're competing with all organic content—not just other advertisers. Top performing ads balance **Entertainment** and **Education** at varying weights.

- Ads that entertain but don't educate → Watchable but struggle to drive action
- Ads that educate but don't entertain → Valuable but weak at capturing attention
- **Top performers balance both**

---

## Entertainment

### Attention-Getting Mechanisms

Recognizable formats, patterns, and triggers that consistently capture attention:
- **Trendjacking** — Ride trending memes, sounds, or video structures
- **Wrappers** — Content formats audience already enjoys (sketch, podcast clip, man-on-the-street) that your messaging inserts into
- **Other examples:** Celebrities, attractive people, cute dogs, mukbangs, ASMR, standup, get-ready-with-mes, prank videos

### Contrast

Humans are hardwired to notice pattern breakers.

**Within the feed:**
- Bizarre or exaggerated visuals (person pouring wine on head, dog driving car)
- AI generative tools help create never-before-seen visuals quickly

**Within the ad itself:**
- Change shooting techniques (tripod vs. selfie, wide vs. closeup)
- Swap locations
- Adjust pacing
- Vary actions from clip to clip

**Opposite Choices:** Consider what normal/expected would look like, then deliberately do the opposite.

### Contradictions

Tension within the same character, object, or scenario by combining qualities not normally seen together.

**Examples:**
- Kevin Hart: Small person playing alpha male character
- The Rock: Massive alpha male who is gentle girl dad
- Cute puppy with green sludgy bad breath
- Sweet grandma with tattoo sleeve
- Baby speaking with deep adult voice

**Why it works:** Brains try to resolve the tension. That "wait, what?" moment hooks attention.

### Social Positioning

**Aspirational Elements** — Reflect who audience wants to be
- Celebrities, influencers, charismatic personalities, attractive people
- Stylish couple in sunlit kitchen, athlete after victorious workout
- Creates heuristic: "If it works for her, it works for me"

**Associative Elements** — Match viewer's own identity
- Creators with acne scars for skincare brand
- Real dog owners in everyday settings
- Creates instant recognition and validation

**Disassociative Elements** — Show what audience wants to avoid
- Often used as "before" in transformations
- Awkward guy fumbling through date
- Church attendee soaked in armpit sweat
- Visualizes nightmare scenario as stick to drive action

### Storytelling

Stories create tension, build anticipation, offer resolution. The Hero's Journey:

1. **Setup (Ordinary World + Problem)** — Meet hero in normal life, something is missing/wrong
2. **Challenge (Call to Adventure)** — New obstacle pushes them out of comfort zone
3. **Struggle (Conflict)** — Difficulties, experiments, failures build tension
4. **Transformation (Resolution)** — Hero overcomes, discovers solution, changes for better

**In ads:** Position product as the turning point leading to transformation.

### Editing for Entertainment

**Sound Effects** — Whoosh, pop, crash give extra sensory cues. Sonic branding (Netflix "ta-dum") reinforces recognition.

**Music** — Drives mood and emotional tone. Common tactic: drop music at 3-second mark when hook lands. Match sad/scary music to "before," uplifting to "after."

**Pacing** — ~25 clips/minute often correlates with performance. Mix fast cuts with slower pauses, wide with close-ups, dialogue with reaction clips.

## Education

### Features, Benefits, and Outcomes

- **Features** — What product is (5GB storage)
- **Benefits** — What product does (holds 1,000 songs)
- **Outcomes** — What it means for consumer's life (running is enjoyable, never run out of music)

**People shop for outcomes.** Start with outcome to hook desire, use features/benefits to build trust in the path forward.

Standard script flow: Hook → Problem → Product Intro → Outcome → Features → Benefits → Offer → CTA

### Mythbusting

Challenges assumptions that are outdated, incomplete, or wrong.

**Structure:**
1. State the Myth — Call out false belief clearly
2. Bust the Myth — Show why it's wrong with proof
3. Deliver the Truth — Replace with new perspective tied to product

**Belief Reranking:** For sturdy beliefs tied to identity, affirm their belief but introduce deeper truth that elevates new belief:
- Bad: "High quality kibble doesn't matter" (counters strong belief)
- Good: "Quality food matters, but gut health matters even more because..." (reranks importance)

### Problem Discovery

Surprise audience with new problem by linking symptoms to underlying cause:
- Symptom: Dog scratches, licks paws, shakes head
- Problem: Signs of gut health issues, not just behavioral quirks

### Solution Discovery

Help customers realize there's a better approach.

**Failed Solutions** bridge into solution discovery: "You've tried expensive kibble, but dog still scratches."

**New Mechanisms** for saturated markets: Rebrand features to sound novel (Lucky Strike's "It's Toasted" — all cigarettes were toasted, but framed as proprietary).

### Comprehension

- Translate complex ideas to plain language
- Use metaphors and analogies
- Balance jargon with simple visuals ("good guys vs bad guys" animation)
- Break into clear steps (First... Second... Third...)
- Use customer's own language from research

### Authority & Trust

Education paired with authority is more persuasive:
- Expert voices
- Founder credibility
- Media features
- Testimonials and data

---

## Balancing Both

- **Entertainment heaviest in opening seconds** when scroll/stay decision happens
- **Editors:** Put bulk of "fancy editing" in beginning to capture attention
- **Once attention secured:** Layer in education while maintaining engagement
- **Alternate** between entertaining and educational moments throughout
- **Capture clips** that exist purely to entertain AND clips that exist purely to educate

---

## Key Frameworks

Use proven frameworks to organize ideas:
- Problem-Solution
- Before/After
- Testimonial Sandwich
- Myth Buster
- Hero's Journey

Psychology amplifies everything—curiosity, authority, social proof, identity, and contrast tap into how people think and behave.

**Interesting ads stop people in their tracks and make them care.**
