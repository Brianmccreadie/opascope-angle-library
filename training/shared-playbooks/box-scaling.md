# Box Scaling & Creative Strategy

## Box Scaling Framework

Scale creative through three dimensions:
1. **Width** ↔ — Expand personas (problems, characteristics, stacked segments)
2. **Depth** ↕ — Expand awareness stages (symptom → offer)
3. **Weight** — Make better ads (testing, iteration)

## Width: Persona Segments

### Problem/Desire Segments (Motivators)
Every product solves multiple problems. Each problem = different segment.

**Gov+ Example Problem/Desire Pairs:**
- "I don't want to deal with confusing paperwork" → "I want it to be easy"
- "I'm worried I'll make a mistake" → "I want to avoid delays"
- "I hate waiting in lines" → "I want convenience"
- "I don't have time to go to an office" → "I want to do it from home"
- "I'm not tech savvy" → "I want step-by-step guidance"

### Characteristic Segments
- Age (55+ primary, expanding younger)
- Life stage (new parents, retirees, frequent travelers)
- Situation (lost passport, expired passport, first-time)
- Urgency (upcoming trip, deadline approaching)

### Stacked Segments
Combine problem + characteristic for sharper resonance:
- "Seniors who hate confusing paperwork"
- "Busy parents who don't have time for the passport office"
- "Frequent travelers tired of long TSA lines"

## Depth: Awareness Stages

### Symptom Stage
**Audience:** Largest, most scalable
**Conversion:** Lowest
**Message Focus:** Connect surface signs to underlying problem

**Script Structure:** Hook → Symptoms → Problem → Agitate → Failed Solutions → Discovery → Intro Product → Feature → Benefit → Desired End Result → Summary Solution → CTA

**Gov+ Example Hook:** "Is your upcoming vacation giving you anxiety?"

### Problem Stage
**Audience:** Large
**Conversion:** Higher than symptom
**Message Focus:** Agitate the pain, show why it matters

**Script Structure:** Hook → Problem → Agitate → Failed Solutions → Discovery → Intro Product → Feature → Benefit → Desired End Result → Summary Solution → CTA

**Gov+ Example Hook:** "Traditional government paperwork is a big headache with different forms, clunky processes, and confusing rules..."

### Solution Stage
**Audience:** Medium
**Conversion:** Strong, people in buying mode
**Message Focus:** Position your solution category as best pathway

**Script Structure:** Hook → Solution Category → Intro Product → Feature → Benefit → Social Proof → CTA

**Gov+ Example Hook:** "There's a smarter way to complete government paperwork without the headache..."

### Product Stage
**Audience:** Smaller, targeted
**Conversion:** High intent
**Message Focus:** Features, benefits, us vs. them comparisons

**Script Structure:** Hook → Intro Product → Feature → Benefit → Us vs. Them → Social Proof → CTA

**Gov+ Example Hook:** "Most services just give you forms. GOV+ gives you everything: pre-filled forms, expert review, and real support."

### Offer Stage
**Audience:** Smallest
**Conversion:** Highest, exhausts fast
**Message Focus:** Final incentive, urgency, risk reversal

**Script Structure:** Hook → Offer → Urgency → Risk Reversal → CTA

**Gov+ Example Hook:** "Get your passport application started today — no commitment until you're ready."

## Nested Messaging Principle

Each stage contains all stages that follow:
```
Symptom Ad: Symptoms → Problems → Solutions → Product → Offer
Problem Ad: Problems → Solutions → Product → Offer
Solution Ad: Solutions → Product → Offer
Product Ad: Product → Offer
Offer Ad: Offer
```

## Format Recommendations by Stage

| Stage | Best Formats | Reasoning |
|-------|--------------|-----------|
| Symptom | Long video, educational content | Need time to build connection |
| Problem | Video, carousel | Need to develop the pain |
| Solution | Video, static, carousel | Can move faster |
| Product | Static, short video | High intent, less convincing needed |
| Offer | Static, short video | Urgency, clear CTA |

## Carrot vs. Stick Language

### Carrot (Positive/Aspirational)
- Dream outcome emphasized
- "Imagine having..."
- "Finally get..."
- "Enjoy..."
- Focus on gains

### Stick (Negative/Fear)
- Nightmare scenario emphasized
- "Don't let..."
- "Avoid..."
- "Stop wasting..."
- Focus on losses

**When to use which:**
- Carrot works for aspirational products/audiences
- Stick works for problem-aware audiences
- Test both angles for each segment

## Creative Fatigue Solutions

### Targeting Fatigue vs. Creative Fatigue
- **Targeting Fatigue:** Same people seeing same ads too many times → Expand audience
- **Creative Fatigue:** Same creative losing effectiveness → Make new creative

### Scaling Priorities
1. **More** — More creative variations (easiest lever)
2. **Better** — Improve based on learnings
3. **New** — Completely new concepts when others plateau

### The Scaling Cycle
- Fresh creative → Good CPA → Scale budget → CPA rises → Refresh creative
- Plan for this cycle in advance
- Always have new creative in production pipeline
