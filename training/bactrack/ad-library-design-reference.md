## 🎨 Brand System — Consistent Elements

- **Signature teal/blue background** (#00B4D8 approximate) used across 70%+ of image ads
- **BACtrack logo:** always present, typically top-left or centered
- **Feature bullet format:** ✅ checkmark + feature text (police-grade accuracy, free shipping, pocket-sized, smartphone pairing)
- **CTA:** "Shop Now" on virtually every ad — zero CTA variation tested
- **Amazon co-branding:** Amazon smile logo prominent on redirect ads
- **Two visual modes:** Branded teal (product hero) vs. dark/lifestyle (video/emotional)
- **Copy tone:** Safety-first but not preachy. "Know for sure" > "don't drink and drive"
- **Platform mix:** Facebook + Instagram, with video ads likely prioritized on Reels/Stories

## 📝 Designer Takeaways

1. **BACtrack's signature teal/blue is THE brand color** — it dominates their paid creative. Any new creative should either use it or deliberately contrast it.

2. **"Don't rely on feeling sober" is their strongest headline** — it's in the highest rotation by far. The anti-guessing angle works.

3. **Amazon is a major sales channel** — they run nearly as many Amazon redirect ads as DTC. Consider dual-funnel creative.

4. **UGC-style video is their second biggest creative bet** — real people, real settings, real BAC readings. The authenticity angle.

5. **Feature bullets are standardized:** police-grade accuracy, free shipping, pocket-sized, smartphone pairing. This is the core value prop stack.

6. **They DO NOT run:** comparison ads, price-led promotions, testimonial/review ads, competitive callouts, morning-after angles, or custody/monitoring ads. These are all whitespace opportunities.

7. **The "Shop Now" CTA is used on 100% of ads** — no variation tested. There's room to test alternative CTAs.

8. **Image ads are simple:** product + blue background + bullets + CTA. The complexity is in the video ads.

9. **Dark/dramatic creative is underrepresented** compared to the teal branded look. Could test more premium/emotional positioning.

10. **Gift positioning and EDC (everyday carry) are minor creative themes** — both could be expanded.
