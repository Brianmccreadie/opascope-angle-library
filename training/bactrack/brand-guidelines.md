## Logo

### Variants
1. **Primary (Light Background):** Full "BACtrack®" wordmark — blue geometric icon mark (stylized arrow/chevron) + "BAC" bold + "track" lighter weight + ® symbol. Dark text on light/white background.
2. **Primary (Dark Background):** Full "BACtrack®" wordmark — blue icon mark (#14ADE4) + "BAC" white bold + "track" white lighter weight + ® symbol. On dark navy/black background.
3. **Icon Mark Only:** Standalone geometric blue chevron mark on dark background. Abbreviated brand identifier.

### Logo Files
- `BACtrack Logo.svg` — Light background version
- `BACtrack Logo (Dark Backgrounds).svg` — Dark background version
- `BACtrack-App-Icon-iOS.png` — App icon

### Usage Rules
- ® symbol always required on BACtrack®
- Maintain clear space around the wordmark
- Do not alter the BAC/track weight relationship
- Do not add colors, gradients, or effects

## Copywriting Guardrails

### ZeroLine® Language
- ALWAYS use "estimates" — never "tells you exactly" or "shows your exact BAC"
- Preferred: "ZeroLine® tells you when you'll be sober" / "shows when your BAC will be back to zero"
- Avoid: "back to 0.0" (not clear enough) / "countdown to zero" (it's an estimate)
- ® required on ZeroLine® in all creative

### Brand Name
- Always "BACtrack" — NEVER "BAC tracker" (reinforces common misnaming)
- Acceptable: BACtrack, BACtrack breathalyzer, breathalyzer

### FDA
- FDA 510(k) clearance is real but CANNOT be mentioned in ads
- Remove any FDA references from advertising creative

### Drinking Scenes
- NEVER depict binge drinking, problematic drinking, or keg party scenes
- NEVER show wasted/visibly intoxicated people
- CAN imply social/party scene — keep it responsible and aspirational
- Think: cocktail party, dinner with wine, craft beer tasting. NOT: frat house, shots

### Driving & Safe BAC Language
- The ONLY safe time to drive is when BAC is zero
- APPROVED: "Make the right call" / "Know when you'll be clear" / "Make the right decision" / "Know before you go"
- PROHIBITED: "Your BAC is low enough to drive" / "Safe to drive" / "You're good to go" (implying driving)

### Forbidden Use Cases
- "Still drunk the next morning" — Do NOT advertise (implies heavy/binge drinking)

### Seasonal Relevance
- Keep creative seasonally appropriate (no tailgating/football ads out of season)

### Xtend® Technology
- ® required on Xtend® in all creative
- Refers to fuel cell sensor technology
