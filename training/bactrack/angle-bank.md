Last updated: March 2026 | Angles: 10 | Status: Initial build

## How to Use This Bank
1. Pick a motivator segment relevant to your campaign goal
2. Choose an angle card within that segment
3. Pair with a characteristic segment from the Audience Segment Plan for targeting
4. Use hooks/headlines as starting points for creative
5. Apply the recommended ad framework
6. Update status: Untested → Testing → Winner → Retired

## 🚨 DUI Fear / Legal Protection

### Angle #1: The $10K Gamble
- **Insight:** A single DUI costs $10,000-$30,000. A BACtrack costs $99.
- **Description:** Position BACtrack as financial protection. Lead with the shocking cost disparity. This is a no-brainer value prop.
- **Best Paired With:** The Social Drinker, The Young Professional, The Weekend Warrior
- **Status:** Untested
- **Hooks:**
  - "A DUI costs $10,000. A BACtrack costs $99. Do the math."
  - "You spent 6 years building your career. A DUI takes one night to wreck it."
  - "The cheapest insurance policy you'll ever buy."
  - "Everyone thinks they won't get a DUI. 71% of people arrested thought the same thing."
  - "$99 now or $10,000 later. Your call."
- **Frameworks:**
  - Problem → Cost Agitation → Solution
  - Stat-Led Hook → Personal Story → CTA
  - Us vs. Them Comparison (cost visual)

### Angle #2: The Morning After
- **Insight:** Alcohol metabolizes at ~0.015%/hour. 8 drinks at midnight = still legally drunk at 8 AM.
- **Description:** Target morning-after anxiety. Massive, underserved creative territory from Reddit research.
- **Best Paired With:** The Weekend Warrior, The Wine Parent, The Social Drinker
- **Status:** Untested
- **Hooks:**
  - "Last night was fun. Are you safe to drive this morning?"
  - "You stopped drinking at midnight. It's 7 AM. You're at 0.04."
  - "Sunday morning. Coffee. Leftover pizza. And a BAC you didn't know about."
  - "8 drinks last night ÷ 0.015/hour = still drunk at breakfast."
  - "The DUI you never saw coming happens on the morning commute."
- **Frameworks:**
  - Day-in-the-Life (morning routine montage → BACtrack reveals number)
  - Myth Buster ("You think you're sober after sleeping it off?")
  - Split Screen ("How you feel at 7 AM" vs. "What your BAC says")

## 🧠 The Perception Gap

### Angle #3: The Eye-Opener
- **Insight:** Customers describe first BACtrack reading as an "eye-opener." The shock moment is the product's natural viral loop.
- **Description:** Recreate the eye-opener in the ad. Show real people guessing BAC, then revealing actual number.
- **Best Paired With:** The Social Drinker, The Craft Enthusiast, The Young Professional
- **Status:** Untested
- **Hooks:**
  - "Guess your BAC. Then test it. You'll be surprised."
  - "You feel fine. You're at 0.09. That's a DUI."
  - "Everyone thinks they can tell when they've had too much. No one can."
  - "I had 2 glasses of wine and blew a 0.07. Two glasses."
  - "Your tolerance isn't as high as you think. Prove it."
- **Frameworks:**
  - Challenge/Reveal ("Guess your BAC" challenge — film real reactions)
  - Before/After Data (feeling vs. reading — split screen)
  - Man-on-the-Street (bar/restaurant guessing — UGC format)

### Angle #4: The Speedometer
- **Insight:** BACtrack's brand metaphor — "speedometer for alcohol" — normalizes the device as standard safety equipment.
- **Description:** Frame BACtrack as standard safety equipment like a seatbelt. Destigmatizes the purchase.
- **Best Paired With:** The Social Drinker, The Quantified Self, The Young Professional
- **Status:** Untested
- **Hooks:**
  - "You wouldn't guess your speed on the highway. Why guess your BAC?"
  - "A speedometer for alcohol. That's it. That's the product."
  - "Seatbelt. Speedometer. Breathalyzer. Some things you just shouldn't guess."
  - "10 seconds. Police-grade accuracy. Know your number."
- **Frameworks:**
  - Analogy-Led (car dashboard → BACtrack screen)
  - Normalization (diverse, responsible people casually testing)
  - Product Demo (10-second warmup → blow → number → decision)
