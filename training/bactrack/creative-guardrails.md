# BACtrack & BACtrack View — Creative Guardrails

Rules and compliance constraints for all briefs, ad copy, and marketing materials.

---

## ⚠️ Legal / Claims Accuracy

### ZeroLine® Language (Critical)

ZeroLine **estimates** when a user's BAC will return to 0.00% — it does **not** tell users exactly when their BAC will reach zero.

**Rules:**
- **Always** use the word **"estimate"** (or "estimated," "estimates") when describing ZeroLine's functionality
- **Never** claim ZeroLine "tells you," "shows you exactly," or "knows" when BAC will return to 0.00%
- **Never** imply precision or certainty — ZeroLine is a projection, not a guarantee

**✅ Approved phrasing:**
- "ZeroLine estimates when your BAC will return to 0.00%"
- "Get an estimated timeline for when you'll be back to zero"
- "ZeroLine gives you an estimate of when you'll be sober"
- "ZeroLine tells you when you'll be sober" ← client-approved simplified version
- "Shows you when your BAC will be back to zero"

**❌ Prohibited phrasing:**
- "ZeroLine tells you exactly when your BAC will return to 0.00%"
- "Know precisely when you'll be at zero"
- "Shows when you'll be back to 0.0" (not clear enough for customers)
- "Countdown to zero" — it's an ESTIMATE, not a countdown
- Any language implying precision or certainty

### General BAC Accuracy Claims (Critical)

This rule extends beyond ZeroLine to **all BACtrack and BACtrack View products and features:**
- **Never** claim any breathalyzer will tell a user their **exact** BAC
- Breathalyzers provide **estimates** of blood alcohol content
- Use language like "measure," "estimate," "read," or "indicate" — not "tell you exactly" or "know your precise BAC"

**Context:** BACtrack has faced false claims lawsuits. All marketing language must avoid any claim of absolute precision or certainty regarding BAC readings or ZeroLine projections.

### BACtrack View & ZeroLine

As of this writing, ZeroLine is a feature of the **consumer BACtrack app** — not a confirmed feature of BACtrack View's remote monitoring platform. If ZeroLine is referenced in View-specific materials, verify with the client before publishing.

## 🚫 FDA Claims

- **Do NOT mention FDA in ads.** Period.
- FDA clearance is real but cannot be used in advertising materials.
- This applies to all channels — paid, organic, email, landing pages.

## 🍺 Drinking Scene Guidelines

### Tone & Imagery
- **Never** depict or imply binge drinking, problematic drinking, or "keg party" scenes
- **Never** show wasted/visibly intoxicated people (especially college-age)
- **Can** imply a social/party scene — just keep it responsible and aspirational
- Think: cocktail party, dinner with wine, craft beer tasting — NOT: frat house, tailgate chaos, shots

### Driving & "Safe BAC" Language (Critical)
- The **only safe time to drive is when your BAC is zero** — this is the only claim we can make
- **Never** imply a BAC level is "low enough" to drive safely
- **Never** suggest any non-zero BAC reading means it's okay to drive

**✅ Approved driving-adjacent phrasing:**
- "Make the right call"
- "Know when you'll be clear"
- "Make the right decision"
- "Know before you go"

**❌ Prohibited driving phrasing:**
- "Your BAC is low enough to drive"
- "Safe to drive" (at any non-zero BAC)
- "You're good to go" (implying driving)

### Use Cases to AVOID in Advertising
- **"Still drunk the next morning"** — Do NOT advertise this use case. It implies heavy/binge drinking. Leave it alone entirely.

### Seasonal Relevance
- **Keep ad creative seasonally appropriate.** Don't run tailgating/football ads 5 months before the season starts. Match the moment.

## 🏷️ Brand Name & Product Descriptor

- The brand is **BACtrack** — one word, capital BAC, lowercase track
- **Do NOT call the product "a BAC tracker"** — people already mistakenly call it that, and using it as a descriptor reinforces the confusion
- Don't write: "this BAC tracker," "the ultimate BAC tracker," "your personal BAC tracker"
- Instead refer to it as: "BACtrack," "BACtrack breathalyzer," "breathalyzer," or "BACtrack [model name]"
- Also never misspell: "BAC Tracker," "Bac Track," "BAC track," "BACTracker"

## 📋 General Compliance Reminders

- All claims should be defensible and substantiated
- When in doubt about a feature claim, soften the language or verify with the client
- "Police-grade accuracy" is an approved brand phrase (per BACtrack's own marketing)
