# Hooks Guide

## Hook Fundamentals

### The Truth About Hooks
- If your hook is bad, your ad is bad
- Hooks filter for who will watch
- The hook's job is to get people to watch the next 3 seconds
- The first 3 seconds determine if people stay or scroll

### Pareto Principle Applied
- 80% of your ad's performance comes from 20% of the content
- That 20% is almost always the hook
- Invest disproportionate time on hooks

## Hook Checklist

Every hook should have:
1. **Visual Hook** — Pattern interrupt, movement, color, contrast
2. **Callout Hook** — Who is this for? (label, question, if-then)
3. **Curiosity Driver** — Why keep watching? (open loop, promise, tease)
4. **Framework Application** — Which of the 21 bullet formulas does this use?

## Callout Hooks

### Copy Callouts
- **Labels** — Traits, titles, places ("Dog moms with golden retrievers")
- **Yes-Questions** — Get people to answer "yes, that's me"
- **If-Then Statements** — "IF you [situation], THEN you [need this]"
- **Ridiculous Results** — Specific, surprising outcomes

### Visual Callouts
- Show the person you're targeting
- Show the problem they have
- Show the product in use
- Show the result/transformation

## 21 Bullet Frameworks (Clayton Makepeace)

1. **"How to"** — Natural curiosity for how-to info
2. **"Secret to"** — Info not commonly known
3. **"Why"** — Building intrigue about why something is a certain way
4. **"What"** — Specific instructions or elusive info
5. **"What NEVER"** — Warns about mistakes
6. **"PLUS"** — Builds momentum at end of list
7. **"Number"** — Groups multiple ways/secrets ("7 signs of...")
8. **"Right... WRONG!"** — Sets up assumption, debunks it
9. **"WARNING"** — Alerts to danger ahead
10. **"Are you...?"** — Gets prospect to say "yes"
11. **"Gimmick"** — Assigns creative name to technique
12. **"Sneaky"** — Element of conspiracy or hidden knowledge
13. **"Statement of Interest + Benefit"** — Exciting fact + strong benefit
14. **"Direct Benefit"** — Strong claim upfront
15. **"Specific Question"** — Intriguing question of importance
16. **"If... Then"** — Conditional scenario
17. **"When"** — Benefit at specific time
18. **"Quickest, Easiest"** — Quick/easy solution
19. **"Truth"** — Debunks myths
20. **"Better"** — Something good vs something even better
21. **"Single"** — Most important piece of info

## Makepeace Makeover (Supercharge Any Hook)

1. **Add Specificity** — "307% profit" vs "big profit"
2. **Introduce Unique Mechanism** — Name the method/approach
3. **Increase Credibility** — Add authority or proof

## Gov+ Proven Hook Templates

### Passport Hooks
- "You are not ready for how easy this is now. So much easier than it used to be."
- "Here is how I got my passport done way faster than I expected."
- "If you have been avoiding your passport because it feels complicated, this is your sign."
- "Getting your passport is so much easier than it used to be... but only if you start here."
- "POV: You finally discover the easiest way to get your passport renewed."

### TSA PreCheck Hooks
- "Send this to someone who refuses to get pre-check. Signing up has never been easier."
- "Lines are expected to be the worst ever this holiday season."
- "Flying during the holidays is stressful with long lines..."
- "Introducing the holiday airport line skipper"

### Tax Hooks
- "Take the stress out of filing your 2025 taxes."
- "Tax season giving you a headache again?"
- "File your 2025 taxes confidently with guided expert support."
- "April 15th is coming! Get expert help with your 2025 tax filings."
- "Warning: If you paid more than $50 to file your taxes last year, you got ripped off."

### Multi/Subscription Hooks
- "Do you want to avoid long lines at the airport with TSA PreCheck?"
- "Do you need a new passport?"
- "Or do you need help with any of these!?"
- "Traditional government paperwork is a big headache..."

## Hook Structure by Format

### Video Hooks (0-3 seconds)
- Lead with strongest visual
- Text overlay matches or enhances spoken word
- Movement in first frame
- Face or product front and center

### Image/Static Hooks
- Headline is the hook
- Visual must stop the scroll
- One clear focal point
- Contrast and bold colors

### Carousel Hooks
- First slide = strongest hook
- Each slide should compel swipe
- Story arc across slides

## Hook Testing Framework

### Variables to Test
1. **Hook type** (question vs. statement vs. callout)
2. **Specificity level** (vague vs. specific numbers/claims)
3. **Tone** (urgent vs. calm vs. playful)
4. **Visual style** (face vs. product vs. scene)
5. **Length** (short punchy vs. longer setup)

### Winning Hook Indicators
- High thumb-stop rate (3-second views / impressions)
- Low drop-off in first 3 seconds
- High completion rate
- Strong CTR
