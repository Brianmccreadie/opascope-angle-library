# SmartTools — Brand Guidelines

> 📋 **SmartTools Brand Guidelines — Quick Reference**
> A streamlined summary of SmartTools' brand identity for designers. Covers colors, typography, logo, and brand voice. Note: SmartTools operates with a dark-first aesthetic and minimal color palette. Additional guidelines may evolve as the brand matures.

---

## 🎨 Color Palette

SmartTools uses a dark-first, minimal color palette centered on deep maroon and near-black tones.

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| **Primary** | `#7A2325` | Dark Red/Maroon |
| **Accent** | `#7A2325` | Dark Red/Maroon (same as primary) |
| **Background** | `#191919` | Near-black |
| **Text** | `#000000` | Black |

The brand relies on a dark-on-dark aesthetic. The maroon serves as the singular pop of color against deep black/charcoal surfaces.

---

## ⬤ Logo

**Logo Overview**
- Dark rectangular mark with a dark-on-dark aesthetic
- Small reddish/warm-toned emblem functions as the brand icon
- Designed for dark backgrounds — do not invert or place on white without approval

**Usage Notes**
- Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the emblem
- Do not alter colors, stretch, or rotate the mark
- The emblem can be used standalone as a social/favicon icon

## 🔤 Typography

SmartTools pairs two geometric sans-serif typefaces for a clean, modern feel.

### Headings: Bai Jamjuree
- Geometric Thai-Latin sans-serif with sharp, technical character
- Use for: Page titles, section headers, hero text, display copy
- Weights: Semi-Bold (600) for primary headings, Bold (700) for hero/display

### Body: Figtree
- Modern geometric sans-serif, highly legible at all sizes
- Use for: Body copy, UI text, captions, CTAs, navigation
- Weights: Regular (400) for body, Medium (500) for emphasis, Semi-Bold (600) for CTAs

## 🗣️ Brand Voice

SmartTools' voice is shaped by founder Nick Colosi's philosophy: make it simple, make it real, make it provable.

### Educational but Not Academic
- Teach the audience something useful in every touchpoint
- Avoid jargon, clinical language, or overly scientific framing
- Guiding principle: "Talk to me like I'm stupid"

### Direct & Unpretentious
- Short sentences. Clear claims. No fluff.
- Speak like a knowledgeable friend, not a brand

### Proof-Forward
- Lead with evidence: athlete endorsements, university validation, PT testimonials
- Every claim should be backed by a real person or real result

### Founder-Driven Authenticity
- Nick's personal story and hands-on involvement is a brand asset
- Content should feel like it comes from a real person, not a marketing department
