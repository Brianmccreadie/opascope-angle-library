# Aroma360 — Messaging Blueprint


> 🌀 Exercise 8 — Messaging Blueprint for Aroma360. This document defines the brand's messaging hierarchy, segment-specific language, and guardrails. Use this as the single source of truth for all ad copy, landing pages, email, and content.


---


# Brand Promise / Core Message

> Precision scenting for every space. Aroma360 brings commercial-grade cold-air diffusion technology into your home — with the design, the catalog, and the control to make any room smell exactly how you want it.
Aroma360 is where scenting technology meets obsessive customization. While Hotel Collection leads with hotel-provenance nostalgia, Aroma360 leads with engineering: a deep product catalog ranging from compact personal diffusers to commercial-scale machines, paired with a pod-based oil system that makes swapping scents frictionless. This is scenting for people who optimize their environment.


---


# Value Propositions (Ranked)

Listed in order of messaging priority.

- 1. Commercial-Grade Technology, Consumer-Friendly Design: The same cold-air diffusion tech used in hotel lobbies, spas, and retail spaces — now in sleek hardware designed for your countertop, not a utility closet.
- 2. Modular Product Ecosystem: Mini 360 SL (refillable), Mini Pro + Pro-Pods, all the way up to Van Gogh/DaVinci/Museum diffusers for large spaces. Right-size your scenting.
- 3. Pro-Pod Convenience: Snap-in, snap-out oil pods. No measuring, no spilling, no mess. The Keurig of home fragrance.
- 4. Refillable Flexibility + Subscription Value: Refillable designs let you switch scents on demand. Subscribe for the best price and never run out.
- 5. Safe, Clean Ingredients: Cold-air diffusion. No heat, no water, no flame. Cruelty-free, child/pet safe.
- 6. Scalable Coverage: From a bathroom (Mini 360 SL) to a 4,000 sq ft open floor plan (Museum line). One brand, every room.
- 7. Sustainability (Supporting): Refillable pods and bottles reduce waste vs. disposable systems. Supporting message only.

# Proof Points & Evidence

- Commercial scenting heritage — Aroma360 serves hotels, retail, and office spaces alongside consumer products
- Pro-Pod system: proprietary snap-in oil delivery eliminates mess and guesswork
- Product range spans personal (Mini 360 SL) to commercial (Museum line) — unusual breadth for a DTC brand
- Oil sizes from 30ml to 500ml — flexibility for light users and power users
- Same cold-air nebulization technology used in commercial HVAC scenting
- Child/pet safe, cruelty-free, no water reservoir (no mold/bacteria risk)
- "Back by Popular Demand" refillable relaunch driven by customer feedback

# Messaging by Segment


## Status-Conscious Homeowner

Wants premium hardware that looks and performs like it costs what it does. Scent is an extension of interior design.

- Lead with: Museum/Van Gogh/DaVinci diffusers as statement pieces — design objects that happen to scent your home
- Support with: Commercial-grade tech in a residential package, large coverage area
- Tone: Refined, design-literate, quietly impressive
- Hooks: "Your diffuser shouldn't look like it came from a pharmacy." / "Designed to sit next to your Eames, not under the sink."

## Travel Nostalgic

Associates scent with places and experiences. Wants to recreate atmospheres, not just fragrance.

- Lead with: Wide scent catalog that lets you curate by mood and memory, not just "lavender" or "vanilla"
- Support with: Refillable system for easy rotation, subscription for ongoing discovery
- Tone: Sensory, evocative, exploratory
- Hooks: "Build a scent library, not a scent rut." / "Rotate your atmosphere like you rotate your playlist."

## Self-Care / Wellness Buyer

Intentional about their space. Scent is part of a daily ritual alongside lighting, sound, and temperature.

- Lead with: Clean, safe, effortless. No heat, no flame, no water. Set your schedule via app.
- Support with: Cruelty-free, child/pet safe, refillable (less waste)
- Tone: Grounded, intentional, minimal
- Hooks: "Clean scent for clean living." / "No flame. No water. No compromise."

## Entertainer / Host

Uses scent strategically — different zones, different moods. Wants coverage and versatility.

- Lead with: Scalable system — multiple units or one large diffuser to cover entire entertaining spaces
- Support with: Pro-Pod ease of swapping for seasonal or event-specific scents, design-forward hardware
- Tone: Social, versatile, impressive
- Hooks: "One scent for the foyer. Another for the kitchen. A third for the patio." / "Your space, your zones, your rules."

## Tech-Forward Home Optimizer

This is Aroma360's sweet spot. They want specs, modularity, app control, and a system that scales.

- Lead with: Pro-Pod modular system, app scheduling, product range from mini to commercial, coverage specs
- Support with: Cold-air nebulization tech explanation, comparison to inferior methods, oil longevity data
- Tone: Precise, enthusiastic about technology, data-first
- Hooks: "Cold-air nebulization. Not ultrasonic. Not heated. There's a difference." / "The scenting system that scales with your floor plan."

# Tone & Voice Guidelines

- Primary register: Modern-technical. Think: the friend who researched every option before buying and can explain why this one is best — without being boring about it.
- Confidence level: Expert and assured. Aroma360 knows its tech better than anyone in the space.
- Sensory language: Use it, but balance with technical precision. This brand sells the engineering as much as the experience.
- Design language: Lean into it more than Hotel Collection. Hardware design is a bigger differentiator here.
- Urgency: Limited edition scents and product drops warrant urgency. Avoid manufactured scarcity.
- Always: precise, modern, design-conscious, tech-literate, confident
- Never: clinical jargon, condescending tech-splaining, cold/robotic, generic "smart home" clichés

# Headline / Hook Formulas

Adapt these templates — never use verbatim in final copy.

- [Size of space] sq ft. [Product name]. Done. → Spec simplicity
- Snap in. Breathe in. That's it. → Pro-Pod ease

- [Number] scents. [Number] sizes. [Number] diffusers. Your call. → Catalog depth

# Forbidden Claims & Guardrails


### Never claim:

- "Medical-grade" or any health/therapeutic benefit from scenting
- "Air purification" capabilities — it's fragrance diffusion, not filtration
- "Eco-friendly" or "green" as a primary message — support only
- "Cheapest" or price-leader positioning — this is premium tech
- That commercial and consumer products are identical — they share technology, not necessarily form factor
- "Smart home integration" unless verified for the specific product (app control ≠ smart home)

### Always include (where applicable):

- "Fragrance oil / Pro-Pod sold separately" when showing hardware-only pricing
- Subscription terms when referencing subscription pricing
- Coverage specifications are approximate and vary by room layout and ventilation

### Tone guardrails:

- Never disparage sister brand Hotel Collection — different positioning, same parent
- Don't over-index on "commercial" — consumers may hear "industrial" if not framed as premium
- Avoid gadget-bro energy — tech-forward but still warm and human
- No feature-dumping — lead with one benefit, support with specs, not the reverse
- Avoid "revolutionary" / "game-changing" — let the tech details speak for themselves
