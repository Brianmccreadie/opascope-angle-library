# Aroma360 — Audience Segment Plan


> 🎯 Brand: Aroma360 | Exercise 4: Audience Segment Plan

Approach: Dual-axis segmentation — Motivator Segments (why they buy) × Characteristic Segments (who they are). Aroma360 occupies a distinct lane from Hotel Collection: more tech-forward, more commercial crossover, and a broader product catalog that appeals to gadget-minded buyers and business owners alike.

Note: While HC leans on hotel provenance and aspirational lifestyle, A360 leads with innovation, precision, and design-object positioning. The segments reflect this — less "bring the hotel home" and more "upgrade your space with the best technology."


# Motivator Segments (What Drives the Purchase)


## M1: The Tech Advantage

Description: Buying the best tool for the job. Cold-air diffusion technology is superior to heat-based, ultrasonic, or reed alternatives — and this buyer needs to know WHY it's better before they buy. The product is a device first, a scent delivery system second.

Key Motivator: Rational superiority and informed decision-making

Messaging Angle: You research everything. Your headphones, your coffee setup, your skincare. Scenting is no different. Cold-air nano-diffusion preserves scent integrity, covers more square footage, and uses no heat or water. This is the technology.

Example Hooks:
• "Ultrasonic diffusers dilute your oils with water. This doesn't."
• "Engineered to scent 1,000 sq ft. Your candle covers 6."
• "The spec sheet on this diffuser would surprise you."


## M2: The Design Object

Description: Buying a piece that belongs on display. The Van Gogh, DaVinci, and Museum diffusers aren't hidden in a corner — they're conversation starters. The scent is almost secondary to the industrial design.

Key Motivator: Aesthetic appreciation and design-forward identity

Messaging Angle: Most diffusers look like medical equipment. These look like they belong in MoMA. A360's designer diffusers sit on your shelf like sculpture — and happen to make the room smell incredible.

Example Hooks:
• "A diffuser you'd actually put on display."
• "Inspired by Van Gogh. Engineered for your living room."
• "Your friends will ask if it's art. Tell them it's both."


## M3: The Space Controller

Description: Buying to command the environment. This buyer wants precise control — coverage area, scent intensity, scheduling. The diffuser is a climate-control device for smell. Appeals to both homeowners and business operators.

Key Motivator: Control, precision, and environmental mastery

Messaging Angle: Set it. Schedule it. Forget it. A360 diffusers let you dial in exactly how much scent, where, and when. Your lobby at 7am. Your bedroom at 10pm. Different scents, different zones, zero guesswork.

Example Hooks:
• "Program your scent like you program your thermostat."
• "Monday: energizing citrus. Friday: warm amber. All automatic."
• "Coverage you can measure. Not hope for."


## M4: The Business Edge

Description: Buying for commercial impact. Scent marketing is proven to increase dwell time, perceived quality, and purchase intent. This buyer runs a spa, hotel, retail store, or office — and knows scent is an untapped competitive advantage.

Key Motivator: Business performance, customer experience, and professional differentiation

Messaging Angle: The best hotels and retail stores don't leave scent to chance. Neither should you. A360's commercial-grade systems scent spaces from 500 to 5,000+ sq ft with consistent, programmable fragrance.

Example Hooks:
• "Your competitors have a signature look. Do you have a signature scent?"
• "Clients stay 40% longer in scented spaces. That's not vibes — it's data."
• "From the same company that scents 5-star hotels. Now built for your business."


## M5: The Sensory Self-Care

Description: Buying for personal wellbeing. Scent as aromatherapy, stress relief, and mood management. Less about impressing others, more about how the space makes YOU feel. The refillable system and oil variety support ongoing ritual.

Key Motivator: Wellness, mood regulation, and personal ritual

Messaging Angle: Your space affects your mood more than you think. The right scent can shift your energy — calming lavender for sleep, eucalyptus for focus, warm vanilla for unwinding. A360 makes it effortless and consistent.

Example Hooks:
• "The 6pm ritual: door closes, diffuser kicks on, shoulders drop."
• "Better than a candle. Safer than incense. Stronger than a reed."
• "Aromatherapy that actually fills the room — not just the 2 feet around it."


## M6: The Smart Investment

Description: Buying on value math. Refillable systems, cost-per-month vs candles, longevity of oils. This buyer has done the spreadsheet (or wants to see it). The refillable Pod system and oil efficiency are the closers.

Key Motivator: Value optimization, long-term savings, and rational justification

Messaging Angle: A good candle: $40, lasts 50 hours, scents one room. A Mini 360: $X/month in oil, runs 24/7, scents your whole home. Do the math — then do the upgrade.

Example Hooks:
• "$3/week to scent your entire home. Your candle habit costs more."
• "Refillable. Rechargeable. The last diffuser you'll buy."
• "We did the math vs Yankee Candle. It wasn't close."

# Characteristic Segments (Who They Are)


## C1: The Gadget-Forward Optimizer

Demographics: Male 28-45, urban/suburban, HHI $75K-$180K, tech-comfortable, likely owns smart home devices

Psychographics: Optimizes everything. Has opinions on router placement, coffee grind size, and speaker positioning. Home tech is a hobby, not a chore. Wants the best-performing product in every category and will research to find it.

Behaviors: Reads product reviews obsessively (YouTube, Reddit, Wirecutter), comparison shops, influenced by spec-based content. Early adopter. Buys DTC. Responsive to "vs" content and technical demos.


## C2: The Design-Conscious Homeowner

Demographics: Male or Female 30-50, suburban homeowner, HHI $100K-$250K, design-aware, likely mid-renovation or post-renovation

Psychographics: Every object in the home earns its place. Buys things that look as good as they perform. Knows the difference between mid-century modern and Scandinavian minimalism. Scent is the finishing touch on a curated space.

Behaviors: Shops Restoration Hardware, CB2, and DTC design brands. Active on Instagram/Pinterest. Influenced by interior design content and product styling photography. Willing to pay premium for aesthetics.


## C3: The Small Business Operator

Demographics: Male or Female 30-55, any geography, business owner (spa, salon, boutique hotel, retail, real estate, medical office), HHI varies — business budget is the spend driver

Psychographics: Customer experience obsessed. Understands that ambiance = perceived value. Wants commercial-grade solutions that don't look commercial. Scent is a business tool, not a personal luxury.

Behaviors: Searches for "commercial scent diffuser" and "scent marketing." Compares A360 vs AromaTech vs Scentiment. Responsive to ROI messaging, case studies, and B2B landing pages. Buys in bulk, wants support and reliability.


## C4: The Wellness-Oriented Buyer

Demographics: Female 28-48, urban/suburban, HHI $70K-$160K, health-conscious, likely into fitness/yoga/meditation

Psychographics: Holistic approach to wellness — diet, movement, sleep, AND environment. Skeptical of synthetic fragrances. Wants clean, high-quality oils. Scent is part of a daily ritual, not just background ambiance.

Behaviors: Shops at Sephora, Goop, Whole Foods equivalents. Reads ingredient lists. Influenced by wellness influencers and podcast ads. Responsive to "clean," "pure," and "therapeutic-grade" messaging. Subscribes to refill plans.


## C5: The Value-Driven Switcher

Demographics: Male or Female 25-45, any geography, HHI $50K-$120K, currently using candles, plug-ins, or cheap diffusers

Psychographics: Not a scent enthusiast — just wants the home to smell good without hassle or waste. Frustrated with candles burning out, plug-ins being too strong/chemical, or reeds doing nothing. Willing to upgrade if the value case is clear.

Behaviors: Searches "best home diffuser" or "alternative to candles." Price-sensitive but persuadable with cost-per-use math. Responsive to comparison content, "I switched from X" testimonials, and bundle deals. First purchase is trial; retention depends on ease.

# Testing Recommendations


## Phase 1: Lead with Tech & Value (Weeks 1-3)

Test M1 (Tech Advantage) vs M6 (Smart Investment) vs M2 (Design Object) with broad targeting. A360's differentiator is tech and design — lead with what separates it from Pura, AromaTech, and candle alternatives.

Format: Short-form video (15-30s) for Meta/TikTok — focus on product demos, tech comparisons, and design beauty shots. Add YouTube pre-roll for the gadget/optimizer audience.

Goal: Identify which rational-emotional hook drives highest engagement and click-through before narrowing audiences.


## Phase 2: Audience Layering (Weeks 4-6)

Take winning motivator(s) and test against C1 (Gadget Optimizer) and C5 (Value Switcher) builds. These are highest-volume cold acquisition segments.

Format: Add comparison carousel ads (A360 vs candles, A360 vs competitors), "I switched" UGC testimonials, and spec-forward statics.

Goal: Find the motivator × characteristic combo with best CPA and conversion rate. Separately test C3 (Business Operator) with M4 creative on LinkedIn and Google Search.


## Phase 3: Expand & Specialize (Weeks 7+)

Roll in C2 (Design Homeowner) with M2 creative — premium design-forward content for higher AOV.
Test C4 (Wellness Buyer) with M5 creative via wellness influencer partnerships and podcast sponsorships.
Scale C3 (Business) with case studies, LinkedIn thought leadership, and retargeting.

Suggested formats by segment:
• C1: Tech review style video, spec comparisons, "vs" content, unboxing
• C2: Styled product photography, room reveals, architect/designer partnerships
• C3: Case study video, ROI calculator landing page, LinkedIn carousel
• C4: Wellness routine UGC, ingredient-focused content, podcast reads
• C5: Side-by-side cost comparison, "why I switched" testimonials, bundle offers

# Brief Generator Tags

Use these shorthand tags when writing creative briefs to quickly specify segment targeting:

Motivator Tags:
• [TECH] — M1: Tech Advantage
• [DESIGN] — M2: Design Object
• [CONTROL] — M3: Space Controller
• [B2B] — M4: Business Edge
• [WELLNESS] — M5: Sensory Self-Care
• [VALUE] — M6: Smart Investment

Characteristic Tags:
• [GADGET] — C1: Gadget-Forward Optimizer
• [DESIGNHOME] — C2: Design-Conscious Homeowner
• [BIZ] — C3: Small Business Operator
• [WELLBEING] — C4: Wellness-Oriented Buyer
• [SWITCHER] — C5: Value-Driven Switcher

Usage example: A brief tagged [TECH] + [GADGET] targets the optimizer with spec-based messaging. A brief tagged [B2B] + [BIZ] targets the business owner with ROI-driven commercial content.

Combine one motivator tag + one characteristic tag per ad variant for clean test structure.
