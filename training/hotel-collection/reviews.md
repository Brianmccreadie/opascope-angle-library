## Negative Themes (Compliance & Objection Awareness — NOT for Creative)

### 1. Customer Service Failures (46.4% of reviews)
The dominant complaint. Slow responses, unreturned calls, ignored emails, chat disconnections.
- "Customer service via phone and chat are unreliable. We have to leave a message of issues and wait 24-48hrs for a response."
- "I emailed customer service twice, including detailed photos and descriptions—and received no response at all."
- "I've been signed up to their membership without knowing. Again asked for this to be refunded. Its a real shame as the scents and diffusers are great. But getting a response on the customer service is ridiculous."

**Implication for creative:** Do NOT promise exceptional service or support in ads. Focus on the product experience, not the company experience.

### 2. Subscription Lock-In (37.7%) & Cancellation Difficulty (34.1%)
Customers feel trapped in long-term commitments they didn't fully understand.
- "36-month commitment" mentioned repeatedly
- "They make it extremely difficult to cancel"
- "Tried to pause my membership... they just continued to send product and billed me anyway"
- "I was subsequently enrolled in a subscription I never consented to"
- "Cancel subscription refill for future orders does not work"

**Implication for creative:** Per compliance rules, any subscription mention in ads MUST clearly disclose commitment terms. Do not position subscription as "no strings attached." Frame as convenience, not lock-in.

### 3. Scam / Fraud Perception (33.6%)
Strong negative sentiment where customers feel deliberately misled.
- "This company is a scam hiding behind slick marketing and influencer promotions"
- "They lure you in with deceitful pictures"
- "Use deceptive practices to enter people into these binding contracts"

**Implication for creative:** Evergreen creative that leads with authentic experience (not too-good-to-be-true deals) will help counter this perception. Honest, straightforward messaging builds trust.

### 4. Product Quality / Durability (21.4%)
Diffusers breaking, leaking, or stopping within months.
- "Both stopped working within one year"
- "The diffusers are horrible as the quality assurance does not justify the price point"
- "Diffuser with a motor only rated for 3,000 hours and a measly 90-day warranty"
- Oil leaking during shipping is a recurring issue

**Implication for creative:** Avoid overpromising on durability or longevity. Focus on the sensory experience, not the hardware.

### 5. Aggressive Upselling (6.8%)
Post-purchase phone calls pressuring upgrades.
- "The minute you place an order you are stalked via phone calls, text and email"
- "EVERY shipment I get the phone calls. Not just one or two, but like 5+ EVERY HOUR"
- "I was sale pressured into purchasing the Penthouse diffuser when I requested the studio pro"

**Implication for creative:** Ads should feel like an invitation, not a sales pitch. Conversational, aspirational tone — never pushy.

## Key Takeaway for Creative Strategy

**The product experience is genuinely strong.** Even the most frustrated customers acknowledge the scents are amazing. The operational issues (subscription management, customer service, product durability) are what drive negative reviews — not the core value proposition.

**This means:**
1. The "hotel scent at home" promise is real and validated by customers
2. Lead with the sensory experience — it's the undeniable truth
3. Avoid any creative that could feel like a "sales trap" or too-good-to-be-true offer
4. Evergreen, experience-led creative is the right strategic direction
5. Social proof from on-site reviews (2,484+ at 4.9★) is safe to use
6. Specific scent names (My Way, Cabana, Paradise, Island Life) resonate and should be featured
