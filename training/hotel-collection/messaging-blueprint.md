# Brand Promise / Core Message

> Bring the lobby home. Hotel Collection lets you fill every room with the exact scents of the world's most iconic hotels — no candles, no water, no compromise.
Hotel Collection exists at the intersection of aspiration and accessibility. The core promise is simple: the sensory signature of a five-star stay, engineered for your living room. Cold-air diffusion technology preserves every note of fragrances reverse-engineered from legendary hotel lobbies — from The Ritz to W Hotels — so the scent you remember checking in is the scent you come home to.


---


# Value Propositions (Ranked)

Listed in order of messaging priority — lead with #1, support with the rest.

- 1. Authentic Hotel-Provenance Scents: These aren't "inspired by" — they're reverse-engineered from real hotel lobbies. No other brand can make this claim with the same depth of catalog.
- 2. Cold-Air Diffusion Technology: Waterless, flameless, no heat degradation. The same tech used in commercial hotel HVAC systems, miniaturized for home use. Covers up to 600 sq ft.
- 3. Refillable Flexibility + Best Value on Subscription: The Studio Scent Diffuser is back by popular demand — refillable design, swap scents anytime. Subscribe and save for the best per-oil cost.
- 4. Safe, Clean, Effortless: Cruelty-free, vegan, child/pet safe. App-controlled. Oils last ~60 days. Set it and forget it.
- 5. Design-Forward Hardware: Sleek, minimal form factor that looks like it belongs on a shelf at CB2, not hidden in a closet.
- 6. Lifestyle Extension: Not just diffusers — candles, room sprays, bedding, laundry pods. A full sensory ecosystem for the home.
- 7. Sustainability (Supporting): Refillable system reduces waste. Mention when relevant; never lead with it.

# Messaging by Segment


## Status-Conscious Homeowner

This buyer wants their home to signal taste, wealth, and curation. They care about what guests think.

- Lead with: Hotel-provenance scents as a status marker — "Your home smells like The Ritz because it literally is The Ritz."
- Support with: Sleek hardware, celebrity collabs, premium positioning vs. Bath & Body Works / Yankee Candle
- Tone: Confident, elevated, subtly exclusive
- Hooks: "Guests will ask. You'll just smile." / "Your home's signature scent shouldn't come from a plug-in."

## Travel Nostalgic

Driven by memory and emotion. They associate scent with specific trips, hotels, and moments. Scent is a time machine for them.

- Lead with: Specific hotel-scent provenance — name the hotels, evoke the lobby, trigger the memory
- Support with: Variety of scents to rotate through, subscription for ongoing discovery
- Tone: Warm, evocative, sensory-rich
- Hooks: "Remember checking into [Hotel]? Now your living room does too." / "Every scent is a return flight."

## Self-Care / Wellness Buyer

Scent is part of their intentional daily routine — wind-down rituals, morning resets, creating a calm environment.

- Lead with: Flameless, clean, safe ingredients. Set-and-forget automation via app.
- Support with: Cruelty-free, vegan, child/pet safe. 60-day oil life means low-maintenance.
- Tone: Calm, reassuring, grounded
- Hooks: "Your wind-down ritual, automated." / "Clean air, real fragrance, zero effort."

## Entertainer / Host

They want compliments. Their home is a stage and scent is part of the production.

- Lead with: Impression management — scent as the invisible detail that elevates every gathering
- Support with: 600 sq ft coverage (whole entertaining space), design-forward hardware, seasonal/collab scents
- Tone: Playful, social, confident
- Hooks: "The secret ingredient to every dinner party? The smell." / "600 sq ft of 'wow, your place smells amazing.'"

## Tech-Forward Home Optimizer

Treats the home like a system to be optimized. Loves app control, automation, and spec-driven decisions.

- Lead with: Cold-air technology specs, app control, coverage area, oil longevity data
- Support with: Comparison to inferior methods (candles degrade scent, reed diffusers are weak, plug-ins use chemicals)
- Tone: Direct, factual, spec-forward
- Hooks: "600 sq ft. 60 days. Zero water. One app." / "Candles burn scent molecules. Cold-air preserves them."

# Tone & Voice Guidelines

- Primary register: Elevated-casual. Think: a well-dressed friend recommending something, not a concierge reading a script.
- Confidence level: Assured but never arrogant. The product speaks for itself.
- Sensory language: Use it liberally. Scent is invisible — copy needs to make people smell through their eyes.
- Humor: Light, sophisticated. Never slapstick. A raised eyebrow, not a belly laugh.
- Urgency: Use sparingly and only when real (limited drops, collabs). Never fake scarcity.
- Always: specific, sensory, confident, warm
- Never: clinical, desperate, hyperbolic, generic luxury clichés ("indulge," "pamper," "treat yourself")

# Forbidden Claims & Guardrails


### Never claim:

- "Clinically proven" or any health/medical benefit
- "Eliminates allergens/bacteria/viruses" — it's a fragrance system, not an air purifier
- "Eco-friendly" or "sustainable" as a primary selling point — support only
- "Luxury for less" or discount-leader positioning — this is premium, not bargain
- Specific hotel partnerships unless legally cleared (say "reverse-engineered from" or "inspired by the lobby of")
- "100% natural" unless verified for the specific oil being referenced

### Always include (where applicable):

- "Fragrance oil sold separately" when showing diffuser-only pricing
- Subscription terms when referencing subscription savings
- "Results may vary based on room size and ventilation" for coverage claims

### Tone guardrails:

- Never punch down at mass-market competitors by name — position above, don't attack
- Never use fear-based health claims about candles ("toxic fumes") — stick to performance comparisons
- Avoid gendered language unless segment-specific creative requires it
- No "you deserve this" energy — it's patronizing. Position as a smart choice, not a guilty pleasure
