## Brand Positioning

**Core Concept:** "Inspired by 5-Star Stays"
**Category:** Luxury home fragrance and lifestyle
**Position in Portfolio:** Accessible luxury ("Gap" to Aroma360's "Banana Republic")
**Competitive Distinction:** Hotel-inspired scent experiences for the home, backed by cold-air diffusion technology

---

## Tone of Voice

### Primary Tone: Accessible Luxury
- Premium but inviting, not exclusive or intimidating
- Think Target's Threshold line vs. Restoration Hardware
- Elevated enough to feel aspirational, grounded enough to feel attainable
- Warm, sensory, experiential — not clinical or corporate

### Tone Spectrum by Context:

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Product description | Sensory + sophisticated | "Sandalwood and leather set a romantic, opulent mood" |
| Social/UGC | Relatable + enthusiastic | "Obsessed with how my apartment smells right now" |
| Evergreen ad creative | Aspirational + inviting | "Walk into your living room like you're checking into the Four Seasons" |
| Gift positioning | Generous + thoughtful | "The luxury gift they'll use every single day" |
| Technology claims | Confident + clear | "Cold-air diffusion technology — no heat, no water, no residue" |

### Voice DO's:
- ✅ Sensory language — describe scents with evocative, specific notes
- ✅ Hotel atmosphere references — lobbies, suites, spas, vacations
- ✅ Social validation language — compliments, impressions, reactions from others
- ✅ "Treat yourself" energy — small luxury, daily indulgence
- ✅ Experiential framing — focus on how it feels, not just what it is
- ✅ Conversational confidence — not salesy, not apologetic
- ✅ Specificity — name the scent, name the hotel inspiration, name the notes

### Voice DON'Ts:
- ❌ Clearance/deal energy — no "HURRY! SALE ENDS!" desperation
- ❌ Corporate stiffness — no "we are pleased to announce"
- ❌ Medical/wellness claims — no "promotes relaxation" or "reduces stress"
- ❌ Overly exclusive — no "only for the elite" or gatekeeping language
- ❌ Generic luxury clichés — avoid overusing "indulge," "opulence," "bespoke" without substance
- ❌ Price-first messaging — per client directive, evergreen creative should not lead with price

## Visual Direction (Inferred from Site)

### Aesthetic:
- Bright, airy, lifestyle-forward
- Hotel room aesthetic — crisp white sheets, marble counters, warm lighting
- Product beauty shots with warm, inviting backgrounds
- More approachable and Instagram-native than Aroma360's moodier look
- Clean, modern, aspirational but not unattainable

### Color Palette (from site):
- Primary: Warm whites, soft golds, champagne
- Accent: Black (for contrast/elegance), occasional rich jewel tones
- Background: Light, airy — cream, soft gray, white marble textures
- Text: Clean serif or modern sans-serif
- Avoid: Neon, aggressive reds, overly dark/moody palettes

### Photography Style:
- Lifestyle-forward — diffuser in a styled room, not just product on white
- Hotel-inspired settings — nightstands, bathroom counters, living room consoles
- Warm, inviting lighting (golden hour energy)
- Human presence when possible — hands, partial figures, candid moments
- Product as part of a curated scene, not isolated

### Video Style:
- Native to platform (TikTok/Instagram Reels energy)
- UGC-feeling content performs well in this category
- Unboxing, room tours, "watch me set it up" moments
- Close-up scent pours, mist dispersal, atmospheric shots
- Before/after room transformation

### Pop Culture Dimension:
- Star Wars collaboration adds a pop-culture/collector angle
- NBA Collection adds a male/sports crossover angle
- Astrology Collection adds a personality/identity angle
- These collabs allow for creative that breaks out of the typical home fragrance aesthetic

## Creative Direction for Ads (Per Client Directive)

### Evergreen Focus:
The majority of creative should be **evergreen** — not tied to specific promotions, sales events, or aggressive price callouts. This means:

1. **Lead with the experience** — How it feels, smells, transforms the space
2. **Lead with social proof** — Compliments, reviews, "everyone asks what I use"
3. **Lead with the hotel story** — The specific hotel inspiration, the travel memory
4. **Lead with the sensory** — Scent notes, atmospheric descriptions, emotional response
5. **Price as supporting, not leading** — CTA can reference price, but it's not the hook

### What "Evergreen" Looks Like in Practice:
- ✅ "Your living room, but it smells like the lobby of the 1 Hotel" (Hotel Nostalgia)
- ✅ "Every guest asks the same question: 'What is that scent?'" (Social Impression)
- ✅ "Sandalwood. Leather. Iris. One diffuser. Your entire home." (Sensory)
- ❌ "$29.95! Was $199! 85% OFF! Limited time!" (Price-led — avoid)
- ❌ "BIGGEST SALE OF THE YEAR" (Promo-led — avoid)
