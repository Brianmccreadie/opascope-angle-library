## Stages of Awareness (Eugene Schwartz)

### Symptom Stage
- People need to connect surface signs to deeper problem
- **Audience:** Largest, most scalable
- **Conversion:** Lowest
- **Message Focus:** Connect surface signs to underlying problem

### Problem Stage
- People recognize specific problem and feel pain
- **Audience:** Large, smaller than symptoms
- **Conversion:** Higher than symptom
- **Message Focus:** Agitate the pain, show why it matters

### Solution Stage
- People aware of problem, researching solutions
- **Audience:** Medium
- **Conversion:** Strong, people in buying mode
- **Message Focus:** Position your solution category as best pathway

### Product Stage
- People comparing products/services
- **Audience:** Smaller, more targeted
- **Conversion:** High intent
- **Message Focus:** Features, benefits, comparisons

### Offer Stage
- People know your product, need incentive to buy
- **Audience:** Smallest, least scalable
- **Conversion:** Highest, exhausts fast
- **Message Focus:** Final incentive, urgency, risk reversal

## Nested Messaging Approach

Each stage contains all stages that follow:
- **Symptom Ad:** Symptoms → Problems → Solutions → Product → Offer
- **Problem Ad:** Problems → Solutions → Product → Offer
- **Solution Ad:** Solutions → Product → Offer
- **Product Ad:** Product → Offer
- **Offer Ad:** Offer only

## Stages of Sophistication (Market Maturity)

1. **Be First** — Simple direct claim works
2. **Enlarge the Claim** — Bigger, better version of claim
3. **Introduce Mechanism** — How it works matters now
4. **Improve Mechanism** — Better mechanism than competition
5. **Identify with Prospect** — When mechanisms exhausted, connect emotionally

## Psychological Principles

### Building Impulses (System 1 / Subconscious)

- **Scarcity & Urgency** — "Only 3 left" / "Sale ends tonight"
- **Social Proof** — Reviews, testimonials, "10,000+ sold"
- **Liking** — Relatable, likable creators
- **Authority** — Expert endorsements, certifications
- **Fluency** — Easy to understand = feels true
- **Mimicry** — Show the action, they'll copy
- **Immediate Gratification** — "See results in 7 days"
- **Reciprocity** — Free value creates obligation
- **Identity** — Help them be who they want to be
- **Anchoring** — First number colors everything after
- **Negativity Bias** — Threats grab attention more than positives
- **Mere Exposure** — Familiarity breeds preference
- **Progress** — Goal gradient effect (closer = more motivated)
- **Framing** — Same info, different spin

### Breaking Resistance (System 2 / Conscious)

1. Identify objections from reviews/comments
2. Prioritize (skip disqualifiers, edge cases)
3. Dig into root cause (5 whys)
4. Insert specific tacklers with trust

### Resistance-Breaking Tactics

- **Belief Re-Ranking** — Don't change beliefs, reorganize priority
- **Gradualization** — Small steps, not big leaps
- **New Mechanisms** — Fresh angle that bypasses skepticism
- **Cognitive Dissonance** — Create tension between belief and behavior

## Fogg Behavior Model

**Behavior = Motivation + Ability + Prompt**

### Value Equation (Hormozi)

**Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood) / (Time × Effort)**

- Increase dream outcome and likelihood
- Decrease perceived time and effort

### Carrot Messaging (Incentives)

- **Dream Outcome:** Best possible result
- **Fast:** How quickly they achieve it
- **Easy:** Reduce perceived effort
- **Likely to Work:** Proof and assurance

### Stick Messaging (Punishments)

- **Nightmare Scenario:** Worst hassles/pain
- **Slow:** Current approach is too slow
- **Hard:** Effort required without solution
- **Risky:** Real risk is doing nothing

## 38 Headline Formulas (Gene Schwartz)

1. Measure the size of the claim — "I am 61 pounds lighter"
2. Measure the speed — "In two seconds Bayer aspirin begins to dissolve"
3. Compare the claim — "Six times whiter"
4. Metaphorize the claim — "Melts away ugly fat"
5. Sensitize (feel, smell, touch, see, hear) — "The skin you love to touch"
6. Demonstrate by showing prime example
7. Dramatize the claim or results — "They laughed when I sat down at the piano..."
8. State as a paradox — "How a bald-headed barber saves my hair"
9. Remove limitations — "Shrinks hemorrhoids without surgery"
10. Associate with values/people prospect wants to identify with
11. Show how much work the claim does — "Relief from all 5 acid-caused stomach troubles"
12. State as a question — "Who else wants a whiter wash?"
13. Offer information about how to accomplish — "How to win friends and influence people"
14. Tie authority into the claim
15. Before and after
16. Stress newness — "Announcing!"
17. Stress exclusivity — "Ours alone!"
18. Turn into a challenge — "Which twin has the Toni?"
19. State as case-history quotation — "Look, mom - no cavities!"
20. Condense/interchange with what it replaces
21. Symbolize with parallel reality
22. Connect mechanism to claim
23. Startle by contradicting expected mechanism
24. Connect need and claim in headline
25. Offer information in the ad itself — "Why men crack"
26. Turn claim/need into case history
27. Give a name to the problem/need
28. Warn about pitfalls without product
29. Emphasize by phraseology/repetition
30. Show how easy by imposing overcome limitation
31. State the difference
32. Surprise that former limitations are overcome
33. Address people who can't buy your product
34. Address prospect directly
35. Dramatize how hard it was to produce
36. Accuse the claim of being too good
37. Challenge present limiting beliefs
38. Turn into question and answer
